Creating a Parenting Plan in Texas That Works for Your Family

Posted: August 23, 2017

Parenting Plan in Texas is required by the courts when parents divorce in Texas. It's common when couples divorce to become extremely contentious during divorce. However, when children are involved, parents must work together to create a parenting plan that serves the best interests of their children.

What is a Parenting Plan?

A parenting plan is a written agreement between parents that outlines how they will raise their children after separation or divorce. In Texas, this document is typically part of the court's final orders and becomes legally binding once approved by a judge. The parenting plan addresses crucial aspects of child-rearing, including where the children will live, how time will be divided between parents, and how major decisions will be made.

Key Elements of a Texas Parenting Plan

A comprehensive parenting plan in Texas should include the following elements:

  • Conservatorship: This determines which parent has the right to make major decisions about the child's education, healthcare, and religious upbringing.
  • Possession and Access: This outlines the schedule for when each parent will have the child, including weekdays, weekends, holidays, and summer vacation.
  • Child Support: Financial support obligations for the non-custodial parent.
  • Health Insurance: Which parent will provide health insurance coverage for the children.
  • Communication: How parents will communicate with children when they are with the other parent.
  • Dispute Resolution: How disagreements between parents will be handled.

The Standard Possession Order

Texas has established a Standard Possession Order (SPO) that serves as the default visitation schedule when parents cannot agree on their own arrangement. The SPO provides the non-custodial parent with:

  • First, third, and fifth weekends of each month
  • Thursday evenings during the school year
  • Alternating holidays
  • Extended summer possession (typically 30 days)

Parents can agree to modify the SPO to better fit their family's needs, but any modifications must be approved by the court.

Creating an Effective Parenting Plan

When creating a parenting plan, parents should consider the following tips:

  1. Focus on the children's needs: Put aside personal differences and prioritize what is best for your children.
  2. Be specific: Include detailed schedules and procedures to minimize confusion and conflict.
  3. Build in flexibility: Life changes, and your plan should allow for reasonable modifications when necessary.
  4. Address all holidays and special occasions: Plan for birthdays, religious holidays, school breaks, and family events.
  5. Consider the children's ages: Younger children may need more frequent transitions, while older children may prefer longer stays.
  6. Plan for communication: Establish how and when the children can communicate with the other parent.

Modifying a Parenting Plan

As children grow and circumstances change, it may become necessary to modify the parenting plan. In Texas, parents can request a modification if there has been a material and substantial change in circumstances. This might include a parent's relocation, changes in the child's needs, or changes in a parent's work schedule.

How America Family Law Center Can Help

Creating a parenting plan can be challenging, especially during an emotionally difficult time. America Family Law Center can help you understand your rights and options when developing a parenting plan that protects your children's best interests while respecting your parental rights.

Whether you are going through a divorce, separation, or need to modify an existing parenting plan, we are here to help guide you through the process.
