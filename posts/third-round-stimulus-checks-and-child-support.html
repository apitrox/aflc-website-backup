Third-round Stimulus Checks and Child Support

Posted: March 1, 2021

Many Americans have heard about the forthcoming third-round stimulus checks that are being proposed, but not how owing back child support or other debt may affect your payment.

What You Need to Know About the Third Stimulus Check

The American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 authorized a third round of stimulus payments to eligible Americans. These Economic Impact Payments were designed to provide financial relief during the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. However, if you owe back child support, you may be wondering how this affects your eligibility to receive a stimulus payment.

Good News for Those Owing Back Child Support

Unlike the first round of stimulus checks distributed under the CARES Act, the third-round stimulus payments are generally protected from garnishment for past-due child support. This is a significant change that affects millions of Americans who owe child support arrears.

Under the American Rescue Plan, the third stimulus checks cannot be offset or reduced due to:

  • Past-due child support obligations
  • Federal tax debts
  • State tax debts
  • Other federal agency debts

How This Differs From Previous Stimulus Rounds

The first stimulus check under the CARES Act could be garnished for past-due child support. If you owed back child support, the Treasury Department could intercept your payment and redirect it to the custodial parent or the state child support enforcement agency.

The second stimulus check provided some protection, but there were still concerns about bank garnishment. The third round provides the strongest protections yet for recipients who owe child support arrears.

Important Considerations

While the federal government will not offset your third stimulus check for child support arrears, there are some important points to consider:

  • Bank Garnishment: Once the money is deposited in your bank account, it may be subject to garnishment by private creditors or through state enforcement actions. Some states have enacted protections, but these vary.
  • Joint Returns: If you filed a joint tax return with a spouse who owes child support, the entire payment may still be protected under the third stimulus rules.
  • Ongoing Obligations: Receiving a stimulus check does not eliminate or reduce your child support obligations. You are still responsible for making regular payments and addressing any arrears.

What About Future Payments?

If additional stimulus payments are authorized in the future, the rules may change. It is important to stay informed about the specific requirements and protections for each round of payments.

Child Support Obligations Remain Important

While receiving a stimulus check provides temporary financial relief, it is crucial to understand that your child support obligations continue. If you are struggling to meet your child support payments, there are legal options available:

  • Filing for a modification of your child support order if your financial circumstances have changed
  • Working with your state's child support enforcement agency to establish a payment plan for arrears
  • Seeking legal assistance to understand your rights and options

How America Family Law Center Can Help

If you have questions about how child support affects your stimulus check, or if you need assistance with child support modification or enforcement, America Family Law Center is here to help. Our team can provide guidance on family law matters, including child support issues.
