Documents You Need to Have to Prepare for Divorce in Texas

Posted: October 25, 2017

Before filing for divorce in Texas it is important to prepare. There are several different legal documents needed for divorce in Texas.

Why Document Preparation Matters

Preparing the right documents before filing for divorce can make the process smoother and help protect your interests. Having organized records gives you a clear picture of your marital assets, debts, and financial situation, which is essential for reaching a fair settlement.

Essential Documents for Divorce in Texas

Here is a comprehensive list of documents you should gather before filing for divorce:

Financial Records

  • Bank statements for all checking and savings accounts (at least 12 months)
  • Credit card statements
  • Investment account statements (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)
  • Retirement account statements (401k, IRA, pension plans)
  • Recent pay stubs for both spouses
  • Business financial statements (if self-employed)

Tax Documents

  • Federal and state tax returns for the past 3-5 years
  • W-2 forms
  • 1099 forms
  • Documentation of any tax liens or issues

Property Documents

  • Property deeds
  • Mortgage statements
  • Home equity loan documents
  • Vehicle titles and loan documents
  • Appraisals for real estate and valuable personal property

Insurance Documents

  • Health insurance policies
  • Life insurance policies
  • Auto insurance policies
  • Homeowner's or renter's insurance policies

Debt Documentation

  • Loan agreements
  • Credit reports
  • Outstanding bills and statements

Legal Documents

  • Marriage certificate
  • Prenuptial or postnuptial agreements
  • Previous divorce decrees (if applicable)
  • Wills and estate planning documents
  • Power of attorney documents

Documents Related to Children

  • Birth certificates for all children
  • School records
  • Medical records
  • Documentation of childcare expenses
  • Any existing custody or support orders

Tips for Document Organization

Once you have gathered your documents, consider these tips for staying organized:

  1. Make copies of all documents and store them in a secure location outside the marital home
  2. Create digital copies and store them securely (password-protected files or cloud storage)
  3. Organize documents by category using folders or binders
  4. Keep a log of when documents were obtained and from where
  5. Update documents regularly as new statements arrive

How America Family Law Center Can Help

America Family Law Center can help you understand what documents you need and guide you through the divorce process in Texas. We can help ensure you are fully prepared before filing and that your interests are protected throughout the proceedings.
